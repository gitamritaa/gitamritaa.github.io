About Śrīla Prabhupāda

His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada appeared in this world in 1896 in Calcutta, India. From his early life, he showed strong devotion to Lord Krishna and deep interest in spiritual knowledge.

He later became a disciple of Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Sarasvati Thakura, who instructed him to spread Krishna consciousness in the English-speaking world. Srila Prabhupada accepted this instruction as his life and soul.

His Mission

At the age of 69, Srila Prabhupada traveled alone to the United States with no money or support. Despite many hardships, he began teaching the chanting of the Hare Krishna maha-mantra.

In 1966, he founded the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), which spread rapidly across the world through temples, books, and festivals.

Literary Contributions

Srila Prabhupada translated and commented on many sacred Vedic scriptures, including Bhagavad-gita As It Is, Srimad Bhagavatam, and Chaitanya Charitamrita. His books are read and respected worldwide.
